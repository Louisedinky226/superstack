## 🌟 The one big idea (read this!)

> **You own the idea. SuperStack owns the code.**

That's the whole deal. Your job is to be clear about **what** you want and **why**.
SuperStack's job is to turn that into real, working software — the **how**. You will
**never** have to read or write a line of code. 🙌

So when SuperStack asks you questions, it's not testing you — it's helping you sharpen
your idea. **There are no dumb questions, and you can never "break" it.** If you don't
understand something, just say so, and it'll explain in plain words.

---

## ✅ Before you start (30 seconds)

1. **Is SuperStack installed?**
   - **Claude Code:** run `/plugin` and check that `superstack` is listed.
   - **Cowork (desktop app):** open **Settings → Capabilities** and check that **SuperStack** is there.
   - Not installed yet? See the [README](../README.md#install).
2. **Start a fresh chat.** 💡 Skills load when a chat begins, so a brand-new chat makes
   sure SuperStack is awake.

That's it. No setup, no accounts, nothing to configure. Onward! 🚀

## 🚀 How to start — your very first message

Just talk like a human. Tell it your idea in plain words. Here are real examples you can
**copy, paste, and tweak**:

> 💬 *"I have an idea for an app that helps dog owners remember when they last walked their dog."*

> 💬 *"Help me turn my idea into a real product, from scratch."*

> 💬 *"I want to start a small business — here's the idea: a website where people book my photography sessions."*

**What happens next:** SuperStack greets you, says **"protocol loaded"** (that means it's
running its full pro process, not winging it ✅), and starts a friendly conversation to
understand your idea. This first stage is called the **kickoff**.

> [!TIP]
> The more you tell it about **who it's for** and **what problem it solves**, the better
> the result. One or two sentences is plenty to start — it'll ask for the rest.

## 🗺️ What happens, step by step

Here's the whole journey, so nothing surprises you. You're in control the entire time.

```
   YOU                          SUPERSTACK
   ───                          ──────────
   💡 "Here's my idea"   ─────▶  🧭 KICKOFF — asks simple questions, plans it WITH you
                                 (problem, audience, the smallest version, the tech, a schedule)
                                          │
                                          ▼
                                 🏗️  SCAFFOLD — creates the project, sets up backups (Git)
                                 and an automatic safety-checker (CI), writes the plan to files
                                          │
                                          ▼
   ✅ "Looks good, go"   ─────▶  🔬 BUILD — builds it feature by feature, tests everything,
                                 reviews its own work. Shows you finished, checked work.
                                          │
                                          ▼
   🚀 "Let's launch"     ─────▶  🌍 LAUNCH — puts it live (website or app store), with
                                 monitoring + backups. Then helps with v2 from real feedback.
```

**You'll end up with:**
- 📋 A clear plan (what you're building, for whom, and by when).
- 💻 A real, working, **tested** product.
- 📂 A tidy project with its own documentation — so any developer could pick it up later.
- 🌍 Something **live** that real people can use.

> [!NOTE]
> A real first version — with proper design and an app-store submission or two — typically
> takes **a few months at a few hours a week**, not a weekend. SuperStack will give you an
> honest schedule during the kickoff. Slow is smooth, smooth is fast. 🐢→🚀

## 💬 How to talk to it

SuperStack makes this easy. A few things to know:

- **🔘 It gives you menus.** Most questions come as a short list of options with a
  **"(Recommended)"** one marked. Just pick the one you like (or type your own). Easy.
- **🙋 Ask anything, anytime.** Say *"why?"*, *"what does that mean?"*, or *"I don't follow"* —
  it'll explain in plain English. Asking is always free and always welcome.
- **↩️ You can redirect.** Changed your mind? Just say *"actually, let's do X instead."*
- **🤖 It only bugs you when it needs YOU.** During the build, it works on its own and comes
  back with **finished, reviewed** work — not a stream of "is this ok?" questions.
- **🛑 It always stops for the big stuff.** Spending money, going live, or anything risky —
  it pauses and asks you first, in plain words. Always.

## ⏸️ Pausing & 🔁 coming back tomorrow

Real projects happen over many days. SuperStack is built for that. 💪

**To pause / stop for the day:** just say a natural goodbye —
> 💬 *"Thanks, that's it for today."*  ·  *"Let's continue tomorrow."*  ·  *"I'm done for now."*

SuperStack will **save everything**: it writes a short log of what you did, gives you the
exact commands to back up your work, and tells you what's next.

**To come back (next day, next week, next month):** open a **new chat** and say hi, or just
jump in. SuperStack reads where you left off and shows you a **menu of your top 3 next
tasks** (with the most important one marked). Pick one and go. ✨

> [!IMPORTANT]
> **It remembers across sessions.** You never have to re-explain your project. If you're ever
> unsure where you were, just ask: *"Where did we leave off?"*

## 🏁 How to end a session properly

When you're done for the day, say a clear goodbye (see above). SuperStack runs its
**closing ritual**:
1. 📝 A quick honest recap (what worked, what to improve).
2. 💾 It **saves your progress to a file** (so next session picks up cleanly).
3. ⬆️ It gives you **one copy-paste block** to back up your work safely.
4. 🔜 It previews your best next 3 tasks for next time.

> [!TIP]
> **Finishing a task is *not* the same as ending the session.** SuperStack keeps going after
> it finishes something — it only does the full wrap-up when *you* say goodbye. So feel free
> to keep stacking tasks in one sitting. 🥞

## 🆘 If something goes wrong — say this

You can't break anything, and you can always steer. Here are the magic words for common
moments (copy-paste):

| When… | Just say… |
|---|---|
| 😕 It misunderstood you | *"That's not what I meant — what I want is …"* |
| 🐢 It's going too fast / too technical | *"Slow down and explain that in simple words."* |
| 🔁 It seems stuck or going in circles | *"Stop. Let's step back and rethink this."* |
| 🔄 You want to change an earlier decision | *"I want to change the decision about [X]."* |
| 🤔 You're not sure it did the right thing | *"Are you sure? Show me how I can check it."* |
| 😴 It didn't start on its own | *"Use the SuperStack skill to help with my idea."* |
| 🧯 You feel lost | *"Where are we, and what's the next step?"* |

> [!NOTE]
> SuperStack is designed to listen for these. If you say *"stop guessing"* or *"are you sure?"*,
> it takes that as a signal to slow down and double-check — not as criticism. 🤝

## 💰 What it costs & 🔒 what's safe

Straight talk, because you deserve it:

- **💸 It will never spend your money without asking.** Paid services, app-store fees, anything
  with a cost — it stops and explains first. It even sets a **spending cap** with you.
- **🔐 It protects your secrets.** Passwords and keys are never saved into the code or shown in
  public. If you ever paste one, it warns you and tells you to replace it.
- **🚦 It will never put your product live without your explicit OK.**
- **💵 Real costs to expect (it tells you up front):** a web domain (~$10–15/year), an Apple
  Developer account (~$99/year) or Google Play (one-time ~$25) *only if* you publish a mobile
  app. Many projects start completely free.
- **🙈 Privacy:** your code can live in a **private** repository that only you can see.

| Symptom | Fix |
|---|---|

## 📚 Plain-English dictionary

No jargon left behind. 🎓 SuperStack explains terms as they come up, but here's a cheat sheet:

- **MVP** — the *smallest* version of your product that's actually useful. You ship this first.
- **Stack** — the set of building blocks your product is made of (the tools and technology).
- **Frontend** — the part people see and tap (screens, buttons). **Backend** — the engine behind the scenes.
- **Repository ("repo")** — your project's folder, with a full history of every change.
- **Commit** — a saved snapshot of your work, with a label. **Push** — uploading those snapshots online.
- **Deploy / launch** — putting your product live so real people can use it.
- **CI (Continuous Integration)** — a robot that automatically checks your project every time it changes (does it build? do the tests pass? any leaked secrets?).
- **Test** — a tiny automatic check that proves a feature actually works.
- **Bug** — something not working as intended. **Debugging** — finding *why* and fixing it.
- **Backup** — a saved copy of your real data, so a mistake is never fatal.

## 🌟 A full example, start to finish

Here's what a real journey feels like (shortened):

1. **You:** *"I want an app where dog owners log walks and get a reminder if they forget."* 🐕
2. **SuperStack:** "Love it. Quick question — is this just for you, or for lots of dog owners?"
   → you pick from a menu. It asks a handful more (who, what problem, what 'success' looks like).
3. **SuperStack:** proposes the **smallest first version** ("log a walk; see your last walk; one
   daily reminder"), and what to leave for later. You approve. ✅
4. It recommends the **simplest tech** (and explains why), shows you a quick **picture** of the
   screens to approve, and lays out a **week-by-week schedule to launch.**
5. It **builds** the project, turning on backups and safety-checks from day one, then builds
   each feature — writing tests first, reviewing its own work, and showing you finished screens
   with screenshots. 📱
6. When it's ready, it walks you through **going live** (and never spends a cent without your OK).
7. After launch, real users leave feedback → it helps you ship **version 2** the same way. 🔁

That's SuperStack: **idea in, real product out — step by step.** ⚡
